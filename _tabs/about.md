## About This Site ℹ️
Alongside my professional work, I actively pursue continuous learning through hands-on experimentation and technical research. This has led me to develop practical skills in secure network design, penetration testing, and adversarial thinking. This blog serves as a space to document technical write-ups and case studies that reflect how I approach security work in practice. The content emphasizes methodical analysis, clear communication of risk, and the connection between technical findings and real-world impact, rather than tool-driven walkthroughs or theoretical discussion.

Here's what you'll find and how it's organized.


## Blog Posts

Writing on topics across offensive and defensive security opinion pieces, threat research, current events, their practical implications, and security guidance for different audiences.

## Case Studies

Walkthroughs of HackTheBox machines and other practice environments. Written up as technical analyses rather than step-by-step recipe lists. Each write-up explains *why* a technique works, maps findings to MITRE ATT&CK, and includes key takeaways that apply beyond the specific machine.

Most case studies are paired with a formal engagement report documenting the same findings in a professional reporting format.

→ [Browse Case Studies](/categories/case-studies/)

## Pentest Reports

Formal penetration test reports produced against the same targets as the case studies above. Written in a professional format — executive summary, CVSS-scored findings, remediation roadmap intended to demonstrate that I can communicate technical findings clearly to both technical and non-technical audiences.

> All reports are produced against deliberately vulnerable practice environments (HackTheBox, ProLabs). Every report includes a disclaimer to that effect.
